Biological Growth Model & Calculus Formulation

In animal husbandry, tracking weight gain is critical. However, animal weight is not linear; it follows a sigmoidal (S-shaped) pattern. We model the growth of a native Malaysian Kedah-Kelantan (KK) Beef Cattle using a biologically parameterised Logistic Growth Curve.

Growth Function \(W(t)\)
$$W(t) = \frac{L}{1 + a e^{-b t}}$$

Predicts the live body weight (kg) of the KK cattle at age \(t\) (months).

First Derivative \(W'(t)\)
$$W'(t) = \frac{L \cdot a \cdot b \cdot e^{-b t}}{(1 + a e^{-b t})^2}$$

Represents the instantaneous monthly weight gain rate (\(\text{kg/month}\)).

Model Parameters (Optimised for Kedah-Kelantan Cattle):

  • \(L\) (Asymptotic Limit): \(280\text{ kg}\) (Mature cow weight)
  • \(a\) (Scale Factor): \(17.67\) (Calculated from birth weight)
  • \(b\) (Growth Coefficient): \(0.21\text{ month}^{-1}\)
  • Birth Weight \(W(0)\): \(\approx 15.0\text{ kg}\)

How Derivatives Revolutionize Animal Husbandry Decisions

1

Finding the Inflection Point

The inflection point is the age where the growth rate derivative \( W'(t) \) is at its mathematical maximum. For our KK cattle, this occurs at \(\approx 13.7\text{ months}\). At this exact age, feed-to-meat conversion is at its peak peak efficiency.

2

Optimizing Feed Costs (FCR)

By matching feed nutrition with the instantaneous value of \( W'(t) \), livestock managers avoid overfeeding. High-protein concentrates are deployed only when \( W'(t) \) is rising, switching to cheaper maintenance diets as the derivative flattens.

3

Pinpointing Slaughter Window

As an animal matures, its growth rate derivative \( W'(t) \to 0 \). Once growth falls below a certain threshold, the cost of daily maintenance exceeds the meat value gained, helping livestock producers decide the precise market endpoint.
